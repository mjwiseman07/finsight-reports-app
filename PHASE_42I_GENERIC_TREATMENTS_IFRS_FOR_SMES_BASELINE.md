# ADVISACOR — GENERIC TREATMENT LIBRARY (42I) — IFRS FOR SMEs
## Framework: ifrs_for_smes | Recommended / In-Review
## Primary baseline: Second Edition (2015), current through 2026; Third Edition (2025, effective 2027) as explicit available alternative
OPERATING MODEL: Advisacor treatments are always recommended
baselines in review — never final; the customer's controller adapts
and locks in at the customer ID. LIB-1 through LIB-8 apply.
output_classification "recommendation_for_human_review" on every
treatment.
FRAMEWORK NOTE: IFRS for SMEs Accounting Standard. Primary baseline
is the Second Edition (2015), mandatory through 2026 year-ends. The
Third Edition (2025) is effective for periods beginning on or after
1 January 2027, early application permitted; where it changes a
treatment it is written in as an explicit available alternative the
customer may elect. IFRS for SMEs is Section-numbered and contains
genuine simplifications from full IFRS — most notably the
operating/finance lease split (T10) and the incurred-loss impairment
model (T11).
## 1. PROFESSIONAL SERVICES REVENUE RECOGNITION
Topic: professional_services_revenue | ifrs_for_smes |
extendsFrameworkDefault | displacementLineage: null
CONFORMANT SPINE (Second Edition — primary, current through 2026):
Revenue from services is recognized under Section 23 by reference to
the stage of completion of the transaction at the reporting date,
when the outcome can be estimated reliably (Section 23.14). Stage of
completion is measured by the proportion of costs incurred to
estimated total costs, surveys of work performed, or completion of a
physical proportion of the service (Section 23.21). If the outcome
cannot be estimated reliably, revenue is recognized only to the
extent of recoverable costs incurred (Section 23.14(e)). Revenue is
measured at the fair value of consideration received or receivable,
net of trade discounts (Section 23.3). This is the IAS 18/IAS 11-
based model, NOT the five-step model.
THIRD EDITION ALTERNATIVE (effective 2027; customer may early-
elect): Section 23 is rewritten as Revenue from Contracts with
Customers on a five-step model aligned with IFRS 15 principles. Over-
time progress uses input or output methods (Section 23.62-23.67). A
customer adopting now may elect the Third Edition five-step model
(early application permitted, disclosed); this routes to advised-
override so the election is recorded.
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Stage-of-completion measure (Second Ed.) — default
cost-to-cost; surveys or physical-completion equally permitted;
customer-adaptable. Outcome-not-reliably-estimable — default
recognize revenue only to recoverable costs (Section 23.14(e));
customer-adaptable. Edition election — default Second Edition stage-
of-completion; customer may elect Third Edition five-step (advised-
override); customer-adaptable.
CITATIONS: Second Ed: Section 23.3; 23.14; 23.21. Third Ed
alternative: Section 23 (five-step); 23.62-23.67.
DIVERGENCE NOTE: Second Edition uses stage-of-completion (IAS 18/11
basis), materially different from the five-step model in full IFRS,
us_gaap, and SMEs Third Edition. Spine reflects Second Edition.
## 2. INVENTORY COST-FLOW AND MEASUREMENT
Topic: smb_inventory | ifrs_for_smes | extendsFrameworkDefault |
displacementLineage: null
CONFORMANT SPINE: Inventories are measured at the lower of cost and
estimated selling price less costs to complete and sell (Section
13.4). Cost comprises purchase costs, conversion costs, and other
costs to bring inventory to present location and condition (Section
13.5-13.8). Permitted cost formulas are specific identification,
FIFO, or weighted-average cost — LIFO is PROHIBITED (Section 13.18).
The same formula applies to inventories of similar nature and use
(Section 13.18). Write-down is required when cost is not
recoverable; reversal is required when circumstances change (Section
13.19).
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Cost formula — default FIFO; weighted-average
equally permitted; LIFO NOT available; customer-adaptable within the
permitted set. Impairment grouping — default by item or similar-item
group each reporting date; customer-adaptable.
CITATIONS: Section 13.4-13.8; 13.18; 13.19.
DIVERGENCE NOTE: Substantially aligned with IAS 2 (LIFO prohibited,
write-down reversal required).
## 3. LIGHT MANUFACTURING COST / OVERHEAD ALLOCATION
Topic: light_manufacturing_cost_accounting | ifrs_for_smes |
extendsFrameworkDefault | displacementLineage: null
CONFORMANT SPINE: Conversion costs include direct labour and a
systematic allocation of fixed and variable production overheads
(Section 13.8). Variable overhead is allocated on actual use; fixed
overhead on normal capacity. Below normal capacity, fixed overhead
per unit is NOT increased — unallocated fixed overhead is expensed
in the period incurred. Above normal capacity, fixed overhead per
unit is reduced so inventory is not measured above cost (Section
13.8). Borrowing costs are NOT capitalizable into inventory — all
borrowing costs are expensed (Section 25.2), stricter than full
IFRS. Joint/by-product costs allocated on a rational consistent
basis (Section 13.9).
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Overhead allocation base — default normal
capacity, consistent driver; customer-adaptable. Normal-capacity
definition — default average expected production over multiple
periods; customer-adaptable. Joint/by-product allocation — default
relative sales value at separation; customer-adaptable.
CITATIONS: Section 13.8-13.9; 25.2.
DIVERGENCE NOTE: Normal-capacity treatment aligned with IAS 2.
Borrowing costs: IFRS for SMEs requires expensing (Section 25.2),
unlike full IFRS (IAS 23) and us_gaap (ASC 835) — a genuine SMEs
simplification.
## 4. DISTRIBUTOR INVENTORY AND FREIGHT
Topic: distributor_inventory_and_freight | ifrs_for_smes |
extendsFrameworkDefault | displacementLineage: null
CONFORMANT SPINE: Costs of purchase comprise purchase price, import
duties and other taxes, and transport, handling, and other costs
directly attributable to acquisition, less trade discounts and
rebates (Section 13.6). Freight-in is included in inventory cost
(Section 13.6). Selling costs, including freight-out, are excluded
from inventory cost and expensed in the period incurred (Section
13.13(d)). Supplier volume rebates reduce the cost of purchased
inventory (Section 13.6). In-transit inventory is recognized when
the entity obtains control (per shipping terms). For purchased-for-
resale goods, no conversion costs apply unless repackaging/kitting
constitutes conversion.
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Freight-in allocation — default capitalized using
a reasonable systematic method; expensing only where clearly
immaterial under a documented policy; customer-adaptable. Freight-
out — default period selling cost (Section 13.13(d)); customer-
adaptable. In-transit — default per shipping terms; customer-
adaptable.
CITATIONS: Section 13.5-13.6; 13.13(d).
DIVERGENCE NOTE: Aligned with IAS 2 and ASC 330 on freight-in
capitalization / freight-out expensing. LIFO prohibition from T2
also applies.
## 5. CONSOLIDATION AND INTERCOMPANY ELIMINATIONS
Topic: holding_company_intercompany_eliminations | ifrs_for_smes |
extendsFrameworkDefault | displacementLineage: null
CONFORMANT SPINE: A parent presents consolidated financial
statements consolidating entities it controls (Section 9). Control
is the power to govern the financial and operating policies of an
entity so as to obtain benefits (Section 9.4) — the older control
definition, NOT the three-element IFRS 10 model. Control is presumed
when the parent holds more than half the voting power, directly or
indirectly (Section 9.5); it can also exist with half or less voting
power through specified arrangements (Section 9.6). On consolidation,
intragroup balances, transactions, income, expenses, and unrealized
intragroup profits are eliminated in full (Section 9.15). Non-
controlling interest is presented within equity, separate from
parent owners' equity (Section 9.13, 9.20-9.22). Subsidiaries are
consolidated from the date control is obtained to the date it ceases
(Section 9.18-9.19). Investments giving significant influence
(associates, typically 20-50%) follow Section 14 — outside this
treatment.
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Control assessment — default >50% voting power
presumption; control with <=50% via specified arrangements requires
documentation (Section 9.5-9.6); customer-adaptable. Unrealized-
profit elimination — default full elimination until realized
externally (Section 9.15); customer-adaptable.
CITATIONS: Section 9.4-9.6; 9.13; 9.15; 9.18-9.22; Section 14.
DIVERGENCE NOTE: IFRS for SMEs uses the older "power to govern
financial and operating policies" control definition (Section 9.4),
NOT the three-element IFRS 10 model and NOT ASC 810's VIE/voting
dual model. Spine reflects Section 9.
## 6. PAYROLL AND EMPLOYEE BENEFIT ACCRUALS
Topic: smb_payroll_and_benefits_accruals | ifrs_for_smes |
extendsFrameworkDefault | displacementLineage: null
CONFORMANT SPINE: Employee benefits are accounted for under Section
28. Short-term benefits (payroll, annual leave, sick leave payable
within 12 months) are recognized as an undiscounted liability and
expense as the employee renders service (Section 28.4-28.5);
accumulating compensated absences are accrued as earned. Defined-
contribution plan contributions are expensed when the employee
renders service (Section 28.13). For defined-benefit plans, the net
liability is the defined-benefit obligation less plan assets,
measured using the projected-unit-credit method OR a simplified
alternative if PUC would involve undue cost or effort (Section
28.19) — an SMEs simplification not available in full IFRS.
Actuarial gains/losses may be recognized in either P&L or OCI as an
accounting-policy choice (Section 28.24) — also an SMEs option; full
IFRS mandates OCI. DB plans are specialist/actuarial — flag,
typically out of SMB scope.
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Short-term accruals — default accrued undiscounted
for services rendered but unpaid (Section 28.5); customer-adaptable.
Bonus/profit-share — default accrued when a present legal or
constructive obligation exists and is reliably estimable; customer-
adaptable. DB measurement (if applicable) — default PUC, or
simplified alternative if PUC is undue cost/effort (Section 28.19);
actuarial gains/losses P&L-or-OCI policy choice (Section 28.24);
customer-adaptable; flagged specialist.
CITATIONS: Section 28.4-28.5; 28.13; 28.18-28.19; 28.24.
DIVERGENCE NOTE: Short-term and DC aligned with full IFRS. Two SMEs
simplifications for DB: simplified-measurement option (Section
28.19) and P&L-or-OCI policy choice (Section 28.24), both
unavailable under full IAS 19.
## 7. FIXED ASSET CATEGORIES, DEPRECIATION, AND REVALUATION
Topic: smb_fixed_asset_categories | ifrs_for_smes |
extendsFrameworkDefault | displacementLineage: null
CONFORMANT SPINE: PP&E is recognized at cost when future economic
benefits are probable and cost is reliably measurable (Section
17.4). Initial cost is purchase price plus import duties plus
directly attributable costs, plus estimated dismantling/restoration
costs where an obligation exists (Section 17.9-17.10); if payment is
deferred beyond normal credit terms, cost is the present value of
future payments (Section 17.13). Borrowing costs are NOT
capitalizable — they are expensed (Section 17.11(e), cross-
referencing Section 25.2), stricter than full IFRS. Component
depreciation is required — each significant part with a different
consumption pattern is depreciated separately (Section 17.16).
Subsequent measurement is by class: the cost model (Section 17.15)
or the revaluation model (revalued amount = fair value less
subsequent depreciation/impairment, Section 17.15B-17.15D) — the
revaluation model is available (added in the Second Edition). If
revalued, the entire class is revalued; increases to OCI as
revaluation surplus, decreases to P&L. Depreciable amount is
allocated by straight-line, diminishing-balance, or units-of-
production method (Section 17.22); revenue-based depreciation is not
appropriate. Useful life and residual value are reviewed when there
is an indication of significant change (Section 17.19, 17.21) — not
mandatorily annually as in full IFRS. Impairment per Section 27.
Gains/losses on disposal in P&L (Section 17.28).
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Measurement model — default cost model; revaluation
model available by class if elected (Section 17.15); customer-
adaptable. Componentization threshold — default significant
components depreciated separately (mandatory where significant);
threshold entity-defined; customer-adaptable. Useful
lives/residual/method — default per documented policy; straight-line
unless another method better reflects consumption; reviewed on
indication of change; customer-adaptable.
CITATIONS: Section 17.4; 17.9-17.13; 17.11(e); 17.15-17.15D; 17.16;
17.19; 17.21-17.22; 17.28; Section 25.2; Section 27.
DIVERGENCE NOTE: Revaluation model available (like full IFRS, unlike
us_gaap). Component depreciation required (like full IFRS). Borrowing
costs NOT capitalizable (Section 25.2) — stricter than both full
IFRS and us_gaap. Useful-life/residual review on indication of
change, not mandatorily annual.
## 8. PREPAID / ACCRUAL CONVENTIONS
Topic: smb_prepaid_and_accrual_conventions | ifrs_for_smes |
extendsFrameworkDefault | displacementLineage: null
CONFORMANT SPINE: Financial statements are prepared on the accrual
basis (Section 2 / Section 3): items are recognized when they meet
the Section 2 recognition criteria, not when cash moves. A prepaid
expense is recognized as an asset when a right to future economic
benefit exists; released to expense as the benefit is consumed. An
accrued liability is recognized when a present obligation exists for
goods/services received but not yet invoiced. Expenses relating to
the current period are accrued regardless of invoice or payment
date. Provisions follow Section 21. Prepaids are current assets if
realized within the operating cycle or 12 months; accrued
liabilities current if settled within that period (Section 4). The
undue-cost-or-effort exemption (Section 2) permits omitting
information where the cost would be disproportionate to the benefit
— an SMEs-specific relief.
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Prepaid release pattern — default matched to
benefit consumption; straight-line for time-based; customer-
adaptable. Accrual estimation — default from known obligations and
best available information for uncertain accruals; customer-
adaptable. Materiality / undue cost or effort — default immaterial
items expensed; undue-cost-or-effort relief applied per Section 2;
customer-adaptable.
CITATIONS: Section 2; Section 3; Section 4; Section 21.
DIVERGENCE NOTE: Practical outcomes for prepaid/accrual recognition
identical to full IFRS and us_gaap. Undue-cost-or-effort exemption is
an SMEs-specific relief with no full-IFRS equivalent.
## 9. DEFERRED REVENUE / CONTRACT LIABILITIES
Topic: basic_smb_deferred_revenue | ifrs_for_smes |
extendsFrameworkDefault | displacementLineage: null
CONFORMANT SPINE (Second Edition — primary): When consideration is
received before the revenue recognition criteria are met, it is
recognized as a liability (deferred revenue) and recognized as
revenue over the service period as the criteria are satisfied
(Section 23, Second Edition). Upfront fees or retainers that do not
relate to a separately identifiable service are deferred and
recognized over the service period. Less formally specified than the
Third Edition contract-liability framework but produces similar
outcomes in most cases.
THIRD EDITION ALTERNATIVE (effective 2027; customer may early-
elect): When a customer pays (or consideration is unconditionally
due) before the entity transfers goods/services, a contract
liability is recognized for the amount received or due (Section
23.68), derecognized as performance obligations are fulfilled.
Contract asset (conditional right) and receivable (unconditional
right) are distinguished (Section 23.68-23.69). Disclosure of
opening/closing contract-liability balances (Section 23.82).
Electing Third Edition routes to advised-override.
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Recognition pattern — default deferred amount
recognized ratably over the service period (Second Ed.); at point of
satisfaction for discrete deliverables; customer-adaptable.
Current/non-current split — default current if satisfied within the
operating cycle or 12 months; customer-adaptable. Significant
financing component — default not imputed under 12 months; customer-
adaptable. Edition election — default Second Edition informal
deferral; customer may elect Third Edition contract-liability
framework (advised-override); customer-adaptable.
CITATIONS: Second Ed: Section 23 (advance consideration). Third Ed:
Section 23.68-23.69; 23.36-23.38; 23.82.
DIVERGENCE NOTE: Second Edition handles advance payments less
formally than the explicit contract-liability framework in full IFRS
/ us_gaap / SMEs Third Edition. Spine reflects Second Edition.
## 10. LEASE CLASSIFICATION AND RECOGNITION (SPECIFICATION)
Topic: smb_lease_classification | ifrs_for_smes |
extendsFrameworkDefault | displacementLineage: null
NOTE: SPECIFICATION of the Section 20 lease model. Actual
measurement/calculation is execution-layer (roadmap RM-EXEC-1).
CONFORMANT SPINE: IFRS for SMEs Section 20 uses the OPERATING/FINANCE
LEASE CLASSIFICATION model (based on the superseded IAS 17) — NOT the
IFRS 16 single-lessee model. Section 20 was deliberately NOT updated
in the Third Edition; the IASB retained the operating/finance split.
A lease is classified at inception as a finance lease or operating
lease based on whether it transfers substantially all the risks and
rewards incidental to ownership (Section 20.4). Finance-lease
indicators (any normally leading to finance classification, Section
20.5): ownership transfers by end of term; bargain purchase option;
lease term is the major part of the asset's economic life; present
value of minimum lease payments is substantially all of fair value;
specialized asset usable only by the lessee. Finance lease — lessee
(Section 20.9-20.11): recognize an asset and a liability at the lower
of fair value and PV of minimum lease payments, discounted at the
implicit rate (or incremental borrowing rate if not determinable);
apportion payments between finance charge (effective-interest method)
and liability reduction; depreciate the asset over the shorter of
lease term and useful life. Operating lease — lessee (Section 20.15):
payments are expensed on a straight-line basis over the lease term;
NO asset or liability is recognized on the balance sheet (the key
divergence from IFRS 16 and ASC 842). Lessor accounting retains the
operating/finance split (Section 20.17-20.28). Land and buildings: a
land lease is normally operating unless title transfers; combined
leases allocate payments by relative fair values (Section 20.12).
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Finance-vs-operating classification — default per
the Section 20.5 indicators; substance governs; operating unless an
indicator is met; customer-adaptable. Discount rate (finance leases)
— default implicit rate where determinable, else incremental
borrowing rate (Section 20.10); customer-adaptable. Land/buildings
split — default allocate by relative fair values; single unit if
land immaterial; customer-adaptable.
CITATIONS: Section 20.4-20.6; 20.9-20.16; 20.17-20.28; 20.12.
DIVERGENCE NOTE: Materially different from full IFRS (IFRS 16) AND
us_gaap (ASC 842). IFRS for SMEs retains the IAS 17 operating/finance
split: operating leases are OFF balance sheet with straight-line
expense. The SMEs T10 spine is genuinely different from the
ifrs_iasb/ifrs_eu/us_gaap T10s, NOT a relabel.
## 11. TRADE RECEIVABLES ALLOWANCE / INCURRED-LOSS IMPAIRMENT (SPECIFICATION)
Topic: generic_smb_ar_allowance_incurred_loss | ifrs_for_smes |
extendsFrameworkDefault | displacementLineage: null
NOTE: SPECIFICATION for the execution-layer impairment engine
(roadmap RM-EXEC-2). This metadata treatment defines the model; it
performs no calculation. This treatment does NOT carry the ECL
forced-recalibration executionConstraints — that construct is ECL-
specific and does not apply to the incurred-loss model.
CONFORMANT SPINE: IFRS for SMEs uses an INCURRED-LOSS impairment
model for financial assets at amortised cost, including trade
receivables (Section 11) — NOT the expected-credit-loss model of
IFRS 9. Retained in both the Second and Third Editions. At each
reporting date, the entity assesses whether there is objective
evidence of impairment (Section 11.21). Impairment is recognized
only when a loss trigger event has occurred. Objective evidence
(Section 11.22) includes: significant financial difficulty of the
obligor; breach of contract (default or delinquency); a concession
granted to the debtor that would not otherwise be considered;
probable bankruptcy or financial reorganization; observable data
indicating a measurable decrease in estimated future cash flows from
a group of assets. Impairment loss = carrying amount less the
present value of estimated future cash flows discounted at the
original effective interest rate (Section 11.25); for short-term
receivables where discounting is immaterial, undiscounted expected
cash flows are acceptable. Recognized by direct write-down or via an
allowance account. Reversal is permitted when a later decrease in
impairment relates objectively to an event after recognition, capped
at what the carrying amount would have been absent impairment
(Section 11.26). The Third Edition adds a required age analysis of
trade receivables (gross carrying amount and impairment allowance
shown separately).
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Impairment trigger — default objective-evidence
assessment per Section 11.22 (loss event required); NOT a forward-
looking aging-percentage matrix; customer-adaptable as to evidence
assessment. Measurement — default PV of estimated future cash flows
at original effective rate; undiscounted acceptable for short-term
where discounting is immaterial; customer-adaptable. Age-analysis
disclosure (Third Edition) — default gross and allowance shown by
age band where Third Edition applies; customer-adaptable.
applicabilityGuard: same healthcare hard-block as the other
frameworks' T11 — patient service receivables are measured under the
revenue standard (Section 23), not under Section 11 impairment;
blocked for healthcare_provider entities; pool-level non-patient
exception with specialist attestation only.
CITATIONS: Section 11.21; 11.22; 11.25; 11.26; Third Edition age-
analysis disclosure (Section 11).
DIVERGENCE NOTE: Fundamentally different from full IFRS (IFRS 9
ECL), IFRS-EU (IFRS 9 ECL), and us_gaap (ASC 326 CECL). IFRS for
SMEs uses incurred-loss (trigger required), not forward-looking
expected loss. The aging-percentage-matrix default used in the other
three frameworks' T11 does NOT apply here. This treatment does NOT
carry the 24-month forced-recalibration executionConstraints. The
healthcare applicabilityGuard is retained.
## 12. ACCOUNTS PAYABLE CUTOFF AND EXPENSE RECOGNITION (SPECIFICATION)
Topic: ap_cutoff_and_expense_recognition | ifrs_for_smes |
extendsFrameworkDefault | displacementLineage: null
NOTE: vendor-miss detection/controller-alert is execution-layer
(roadmap RM-EXEC-3). This metadata treatment defines the cutoff
baseline.
CONFORMANT SPINE: Under the accrual basis (Section 2 / Section 3),
expenses are recognized in the period the economic benefit is
consumed (goods/services received), regardless of when the vendor
invoice is received, entered, or paid. Goods received before period-
end create a payable even if the invoice has not arrived (goods-
received-not-invoiced); services performed before period-end by
third parties create an accrued liability. At period end, liabilities
are accrued for goods and services received but not yet invoiced or
entered. An invoice dated in one period but entered in a subsequent
period does not move the expense — it belongs to the period of
receipt; proper cutoff requires accruing it in the correct period.
Provisions and accruals follow Section 21.
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Accrual cutoff — default at each period close,
accrue for received-but-unentered goods/services from receiving
records (GRNI) and known obligations; customer-adaptable.
Materiality / undue cost or effort — default cutoff accruals above an
entity-defined threshold; undue-cost-or-effort relief per Section 2;
customer-adaptable.
EXECUTION SPEC (roadmap RM-EXEC-3): a monitoring capability should
detect invoices whose invoice date precedes their entry period,
track recurring missed vendors, and alert the controller with the
reason — human review, not auto-posting.
CITATIONS: Section 2; Section 3; Section 21.
DIVERGENCE NOTE: Accrual and cutoff principles substantially aligned
with full IFRS and us_gaap.
