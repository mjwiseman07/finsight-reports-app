# ADVISACOR — GENERIC TREATMENT LIBRARY (42I) — IFRS AS ADOPTED BY THE EU
## Framework: ifrs_eu | Recommended / In-Review
OPERATING MODEL: Advisacor treatments are always recommended
baselines in review on Advisacor's side — never final. The
customer's controller reviews, adapts, and locks in at the customer
ID; the customer takes responsibility for the model they sign off
on. LIB-1 through LIB-8 apply. output_classification
"recommendation_for_human_review" on every treatment.
FRAMEWORK NOTE: IFRS as adopted by the EU (IFRS-EU) per Regulation
(EC) No 1606/2002, consolidated in Commission Regulation (EU)
2023/1803. For all twelve SMB topics in this library, IFRS-EU is
IDENTICAL to full IASB IFRS — every relevant standard (IFRS 15, IAS
2, IFRS 10, IAS 19, IAS 16, IFRS 16, IFRS 9, IAS 1, IAS 37) is
endorsed without modification. The two EU-specific divergences from
full IFRS (the IAS 39 macro-hedge carve-out and the non-endorsement
of the 2018 Conceptual Framework) do not affect these SMB topics
substantively; they are noted explicitly in Treatments 8 and 11.
## 1. PROFESSIONAL SERVICES REVENUE RECOGNITION
Topic: professional_services_revenue | ifrs_eu |
extendsFrameworkDefault | displacementLineage: null
CONFORMANT SPINE: Revenue from professional services is recognized
under IFRS 15's five-step model in the amount the entity expects to
be entitled to (IFRS 15.2). IFRS 15 is endorsed under IFRS-EU
without modification (Commission Regulation (EU) 2016/1905;
effective 1 January 2018) — identical to full IASB IFRS. A
performance obligation is satisfied over time if any IFRS 15.35
criterion is met: (a) customer simultaneously receives and consumes
benefits; (b) performance creates/enhances an asset the customer
controls; (c) performance creates an asset with no alternative use
AND the entity has an enforceable right to payment for performance
to date. If no criterion is met, revenue is recognized at the point
control transfers (IFRS 15.38). Over-time progress is measured by a
single output or input method (IFRS 15.39-45). Variable
consideration is estimated and constrained where significant
reversal is probable (IFRS 15.50-58). Stand-ready/retainer
arrangements that are a series of substantially-the-same distinct
services with the same progress measure are a single performance
obligation. Onerous performance obligations: a provision is
recognized under IAS 37.66-67 (no ASC 606 parallel).
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Measure of progress — default input method
(costs/hours) where it best depicts performance; output methods
equally permitted; consistent policy disclosed (IFRS 15.123);
customer-adaptable. Variable consideration (conservative) — default
contingent/variable fees constrained to zero until the constraint
is lifted; earlier recognition routes to advised-override; customer-
adaptable. One-year expedients (IFRS 15.94; 15.63) — default
applied; customer may elect not to; customer-adaptable.
CITATIONS: IFRS 15.2; 15.9; 15.22-30; 15.35; 15.38; 15.39-45;
15.50-58; 15.63; 15.91-104; 15.123; IAS 37.66-67. EU endorsement:
Commission Regulation (EU) 2016/1905.
EU DIVERGENCE FROM FULL IFRS: None — IFRS 15 endorsed without
modification.
## 2. INVENTORY COST-FLOW AND MEASUREMENT
Topic: smb_inventory | ifrs_eu | extendsFrameworkDefault |
displacementLineage: null
CONFORMANT SPINE: Inventories are measured at the lower of cost and
net realisable value (IAS 2.9). IAS 2 is endorsed under IFRS-EU
without modification — identical to full IASB IFRS. Permitted cost
formulas are specific identification, FIFO, or weighted-average cost
only — LIFO is PROHIBITED (IAS 2.25). NRV is selling price less
costs of completion and costs to sell (IAS 2.6), assessed item-by-
item or by similar groups (IAS 2.28-29). Reversal of a prior write-
down is REQUIRED when NRV recovers, limited to the original write-
down amount (IAS 2.33).
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Cost formula — default FIFO; weighted-average
equally permitted; LIFO NOT available; customer-adaptable within the
permitted set. NRV grouping — default by item or similar-item group
each reporting date (IAS 2.29); customer-adaptable. Costing
technique — default standard or retail where it approximates actual
cost (IAS 2.21-22); customer-adaptable.
CITATIONS: IAS 2.6; 2.9-22; 2.25-29; 2.33-34; 2.36. EU endorsement:
Commission Regulation (EU) 2023/1803.
EU DIVERGENCE FROM FULL IFRS: None — IAS 2 endorsed without
modification; LIFO prohibition applies equally.
## 3. LIGHT MANUFACTURING COST / OVERHEAD ALLOCATION
Topic: light_manufacturing_cost_accounting | ifrs_eu |
extendsFrameworkDefault | displacementLineage: null
CONFORMANT SPINE: Costs of conversion include direct labour and a
systematic allocation of fixed and variable production overheads
(IAS 2.12). IAS 2 endorsed under IFRS-EU without modification.
Variable overhead is allocated on actual use; fixed overhead on
normal capacity — production expected on average over a number of
periods net of planned-maintenance capacity loss (IAS 2.13). Below
normal capacity, fixed overhead per unit is NOT increased;
unabsorbed fixed overhead is expensed in the period incurred (IAS
2.13). Above normal capacity, fixed overhead per unit is reduced so
inventory is not measured above cost. Joint costs allocated on a
rational consistent basis; by-products typically at NRV deducted
from the main product (IAS 2.14). Service-business inventory
comprises directly-engaged labour and attributable overhead,
excluding selling/admin (IAS 2.19).
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Normal-capacity determination — default average
expected production over multiple periods net of planned
maintenance; entity-defined; customer-adaptable. Costing system —
default actual or standard where standard approximates actual (IAS
2.21); customer-adaptable. Joint/by-product allocation — default
relative sales value at separation; by-products at NRV; customer-
adaptable.
CITATIONS: IAS 2.12-19; 2.21. EU endorsement: Commission Regulation
(EU) 2023/1803.
EU DIVERGENCE FROM FULL IFRS: None.
## 4. DISTRIBUTOR INVENTORY AND FREIGHT
Topic: distributor_inventory_and_freight | ifrs_eu |
extendsFrameworkDefault | displacementLineage: null
CONFORMANT SPINE: Purchased inventory cost includes purchase price,
import duties, non-recoverable taxes, transport/handling and other
directly attributable acquisition costs, less trade discounts and
rebates (IAS 2.11). IAS 2 endorsed under IFRS-EU without
modification. Freight-in is part of cost of purchase. Selling costs
including freight-out are excluded from inventory cost (IAS 2.16)
and are period costs. Inventory at lower of cost and NRV (IAS 2.9).
In-transit inventory recognized per contractual transfer of
control/risks and rewards.
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Freight-in allocation — default capitalized using
a reasonable systematic method; expensing only where clearly
immaterial under a documented policy; customer-adaptable. Freight-
out — default period selling cost (IAS 2.16); customer-adaptable.
In-transit — default per shipping terms; customer-adaptable.
CITATIONS: IAS 2.10-11; 2.16; 2.9. EU endorsement: Commission
Regulation (EU) 2023/1803.
EU DIVERGENCE FROM FULL IFRS: None.
## 5. CONSOLIDATION AND INTERCOMPANY ELIMINATIONS
Topic: holding_company_intercompany_eliminations | ifrs_eu |
extendsFrameworkDefault | displacementLineage: null
CONFORMANT SPINE: An investor consolidates an investee it controls.
IFRS 10 is endorsed under IFRS-EU (Commission Regulation (EU)
1254/2012, with the one-year deferred mandatory effective date of 1
January 2014 in the EU) and applies a SINGLE control-based model
(IFRS 10.4-7) — NO variable-interest-entity concept and no dual
model as under us_gaap. Control requires all three elements: (a)
power over the investee; (b) exposure to variable returns; (c)
ability to use power to affect those returns (IFRS 10.7). De facto
control is explicitly recognized — a large minority holder may
control where other holdings are dispersed (IFRS 10.B41-B46), so
consolidation can be required below 50% ownership. Potential voting
rights currently exercisable are considered (IFRS 10.B47-B50).
Principal-vs-agent analysis applies (IFRS 10.B58-B72). On
consolidation, all intragroup balances, transactions, and unrealised
intragroup profit in assets are eliminated in full (IFRS 10.B86(c)).
NCI is presented within equity (IFRS 10.B94). Significant-influence
interests (typically 20-50%) follow the equity method (IAS 28) —
outside this treatment.
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Control assessment — default three-element test;
de-facto-control, potential-voting-rights, principal/agent are
entity-specific judgments requiring documentation; customer-
adaptable. Investment-entity exception (IFRS 10.27-33) — default not
applied unless the entity qualifies; customer-adaptable.
Unrealised-profit elimination — default full elimination until
realised externally; customer-adaptable.
CITATIONS: IFRS 10.4-7; 10.27-33; 10.B41-B50; 10.B58-B72; 10.B86;
10.B94; IAS 28. EU endorsement: Commission Regulation (EU)
1254/2012.
EU DIVERGENCE FROM FULL IFRS: None on the control model. (Historical
note: EU mandatory effective date was deferred one year to 1 January
2014 vs IASB's 2013; no longer relevant.)
## 6. PAYROLL AND EMPLOYEE BENEFIT ACCRUALS
Topic: smb_payroll_and_benefits_accruals | ifrs_eu |
extendsFrameworkDefault | displacementLineage: null
CONFORMANT SPINE: Employee benefits are accounted for under IAS 19
(2011 revision, endorsed via Commission Regulation (EU) 475/2012;
share-based payment is IFRS 2, outside this treatment). Short-term
benefits (settled wholly within 12 months) are recognized as an
undiscounted liability for services rendered but unpaid, and as
expense unless capitalized into an asset (IAS 19.11). Accumulating
compensated absences are accrued as employees render service
increasing entitlement (IAS 19.13); non-accumulating when the
absence occurs (IAS 19.17). Profit-sharing and bonuses are
recognized when a present legal or constructive obligation exists
and a reliable estimate can be made (IAS 19.19-20). Post-employment
plans are classified defined-contribution (expense as service is
rendered, IAS 19.51) or defined-benefit (projected-unit-credit;
remeasurements in OCI never recycled; past service cost expensed
immediately; the corridor approach is PROHIBITED since 1 January
2013 — IAS 19.55-152). DB and other long-term benefits are
specialist/actuarial — flag, typically out of SMB scope.
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Short-term accruals — default accrued undiscounted
(IAS 19.11); customer-adaptable. Bonus/profit-share — default
accrued when a legal or constructive obligation exists and is
reliably estimable (IAS 19.20); customer-adaptable. Compensated
absences — default accumulating accrued as entitlement grows;
customer-adaptable. DB plans — default flagged actuarial/specialist;
customer-adaptable.
CITATIONS: IAS 19.8-25; 19.11; 19.13-24; 19.51-54; 19.55-152; IFRS
2. EU endorsement: Commission Regulation (EU) 475/2012.
EU DIVERGENCE FROM FULL IFRS: None — IAS 19 (2011) endorsed without
modification; corridor approach prohibited equally since 2013.
## 7. FIXED ASSET CATEGORIES, DEPRECIATION, AND REVALUATION
Topic: smb_fixed_asset_categories | ifrs_eu |
extendsFrameworkDefault | displacementLineage: null
CONFORMANT SPINE: PP&E is recognized at cost when future economic
benefits are probable and cost is reliably measurable (IAS 16.7,
16.15). IAS 16 endorsed under IFRS-EU without modification.
Component depreciation is MANDATORY — each significant part with a
different useful life or consumption pattern is depreciated
separately (IAS 16.43). Subsequent measurement uses, by class, the
cost model (IAS 16.30) or the revaluation model (IAS 16.31) — the
revaluation model is PERMITTED under IFRS-EU; if elected, the entire
class must be revalued (IAS 16.36), increases to OCI as revaluation
surplus, decreases to P&L (IAS 16.39-40). Proceeds from selling
items produced before PP&E is available for use are recognized in
P&L, not deducted from cost (IAS 16.17A, 2020 amendment, endorsed).
Depreciable amount is allocated over useful life; residual value and
useful life reviewed at least annually (IAS 16.50-51). Land is not
depreciated (IAS 16.58). Impairment under IAS 36: write down to
recoverable amount (higher of value-in-use and fair value less costs
to sell, IAS 36.6, 36.60). Impairment reversals are PERMITTED up to
the amount that would have been recognized absent impairment (IAS
36.114).
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Measurement model — default cost model;
revaluation model available by class if elected (IAS 16.29);
customer-adaptable. Componentization threshold — default components
above an entity-defined significance threshold (mandatory where
significant); customer-adaptable as to threshold. Useful
lives/residual/method — default per documented policy; reviewed
annually; customer-adaptable.
CITATIONS: IAS 16.7; 16.15-17A; 16.29-42; 16.43-47; 16.50-62; 16.58;
IAS 36.6; 36.60; 36.114; IAS 23. EU endorsement: Commission
Regulation (EU) 2023/1803.
EU DIVERGENCE FROM FULL IFRS: None — both cost and revaluation models
equally available; component depreciation mandatory equally.
## 8. PREPAID / ACCRUAL CONVENTIONS
Topic: smb_prepaid_and_accrual_conventions | ifrs_eu |
extendsFrameworkDefault | displacementLineage: null
CONFORMANT SPINE: Financial statements (except cash flow
information) are prepared on the accrual basis, mandated by IAS
1.27-28 (endorsed under IFRS-EU). A prepaid expense is recognized as
an asset (a right to future economic benefit) and released to
expense as the benefit is consumed; the operative authority is the
endorsed standards (IAS 1, and IAS 38 for intangible prepayments,
IAS 16 for PP&E deposits). An accrued liability is recognized when a
present obligation exists for goods/services received but not yet
invoiced (IAS 1.27; IAS 37.14 for provisions). Expenses relating to
the current period are accrued regardless of invoice or payment date
(IAS 1.28). Prepaids are current assets if realized within the
operating cycle or 12 months; accrued liabilities current if settled
within the operating cycle or 12 months (IAS 1.54-55).
EU-SPECIFIC NOTE: The 2018 IASB Conceptual Framework is NOT endorsed
under IFRS-EU and is not part of IFRS-EU. Accrual and prepaid
treatment therefore derives directly from the endorsed standards
(IAS 1, IAS 37), not from the Conceptual Framework. Per IAS 8.11(b)
(endorsed), the unendorsed Conceptual Framework may be referred to
only as a development tool where no endorsed standard applies; it
does not override any endorsed standard. This non-endorsement has no
practical impact on prepaid/accrual accounting.
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Prepaid release pattern — default matched to
benefit consumption; straight-line for time-based (IAS 1.28);
customer-adaptable. Accrual estimation — default from known
obligations and run-rate for unbilled items; customer-adaptable.
Materiality threshold — default above an entity-defined threshold
(IAS 1.7); customer-adaptable.
CITATIONS: IAS 1.7; 1.27-28; 1.54-55; IAS 8.11; IAS 37.14; IAS 19.11.
EU endorsement: Commission Regulation (EU) 2023/1803.
EU DIVERGENCE FROM FULL IFRS: The 2018 Conceptual Framework is not
EU-endorsed (active divergence), but it has no practical effect here
because the operative rules are in the endorsed standards. Spine
cites IAS 1/IAS 37 directly rather than the Conceptual Framework.
## 9. DEFERRED REVENUE / CONTRACT LIABILITIES
Topic: basic_smb_deferred_revenue | ifrs_eu |
extendsFrameworkDefault | displacementLineage: null
CONFORMANT SPINE: Under IFRS 15 (endorsed without modification), the
balance sheet presents a contract liability or contract asset
depending on the relationship between performance and payment (IFRS
15.105). A contract liability (the IFRS-15 term for deferred
revenue) is the entity's obligation to transfer goods/services for
which it has received or is due consideration (IFRS 15.106). A
contract asset is a conditional right to consideration, distinguished
from a trade receivable, which is an unconditional right requiring
only passage of time (IFRS 15.107-108). Within a single contract, a
contract asset and contract liability are presented net (IFRS
15.105). The contract liability is released to revenue as the
related performance obligation is satisfied (IFRS 15.31-38). Contract
assets are subject to IFRS 9 ECL impairment (IFRS 15.107; IFRS
9.5.5.15 — see Treatment 11). Significant financing component
applies where payment is materially in advance, with the one-year
expedient (IFRS 15.60-65).
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Recognition pattern — default released ratably for
stand-ready/subscription, at point of satisfaction for discrete;
customer-adaptable. Balance-sheet aggregation — default net per
contract; customer-adaptable. Significant financing component —
default not imputed under 12 months (15.63); customer-adaptable.
CITATIONS: IFRS 15.105-108; 15.113; 15.116; 15.120-122; 15.60-65;
IFRS 9.5.5.15. EU endorsement: Commission Regulation (EU) 2016/1905.
EU DIVERGENCE FROM FULL IFRS: None.
## 10. LEASE CLASSIFICATION AND RECOGNITION (SPECIFICATION)
Topic: smb_lease_classification | ifrs_eu | extendsFrameworkDefault |
displacementLineage: null
NOTE: SPECIFICATION of the IFRS 16 recognition model. Actual
measurement/calculation is execution-layer (roadmap RM-EXEC-1).
CONFORMANT SPINE: IFRS 16 (endorsed via Commission Regulation (EU)
2017/1986, effective 1 January 2019, without modification) applies a
SINGLE on-balance-sheet lessee model — NO operating-vs-finance
classification for lessees (unlike us_gaap's dual model). A lessee
recognizes a right-of-use asset and a lease liability for all leases
over 12 months, subject to exemptions (IFRS 16.22). Two optional
exemptions by class/lease: short-term leases (<=12 months, IFRS
16.5(a)) and low-value assets (BC references ~USD 5,000 when new,
IFRS 16.5(b)). The lease liability is the present value of unpaid
lease payments at the rate implicit in the lease, or the incremental
borrowing rate if not determinable (IFRS 16.26-27). The ROU asset is
the liability plus payments at/before commencement, plus initial
direct costs and restoration costs, less incentives (IFRS 16.24).
Subsequently the liability accretes interest and reduces for
payments; the ROU asset is depreciated over the shorter of lease
term and asset useful life (IFRS 16.29-33). The income-statement
effect is front-loaded for all lessee leases. Lessors retain
finance/operating classification (IFRS 16.61-97). Modifications (IFRS
16.44-46) and sale-and-leaseback (IFRS 16.98-103, 2024 amendment,
endorsed) apply.
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Lease-term/reasonably-certain options — default per
IFRS 16.19; customer-adaptable. Short-term and low-value exemptions —
default available; customer elects by asset class; customer-
adaptable. Discount rate — default implicit rate where determinable
else incremental borrowing rate (IFRS 16.26); customer-adaptable.
Lease-vs-service/identified asset — default per IFRS 16.9-11;
customer-adaptable.
CITATIONS: IFRS 16.5; 16.9-11; 16.19; 16.22-33; 16.44-46; 16.61-97;
16.98-103. EU endorsement: Commission Regulation (EU) 2017/1986.
EU DIVERGENCE FROM FULL IFRS: None.
## 11. TRADE RECEIVABLES ALLOWANCE / EXPECTED CREDIT LOSSES (SPECIFICATION)
Topic: generic_smb_ar_allowance_ecl | ifrs_eu |
extendsFrameworkDefault | displacementLineage: null
NOTE: SPECIFICATION for the execution-layer ECL computation engine
(roadmap RM-EXEC-2). This metadata treatment defines the model; it
performs no calculation.
CONFORMANT SPINE: Trade receivables and contract assets carry an
allowance for expected credit losses under IFRS 9 (endorsed via
Commission Regulation (EU) 2016/2067). IFRS 9 uses a forward-looking
expected-credit-loss model. For trade receivables (and contract
assets) without a significant financing component, the SIMPLIFIED
APPROACH is required — lifetime ECL is recognized from initial
recognition, with no staging assessment (IFRS 9.5.5.15). A provision
matrix (loss rates by aging bucket, derived from historical loss
experience and adjusted for forward-looking/reasonable-and-
supportable information) is the standard practical method. The
allowance reduces the receivable to its expected-recoverable amount.
EU-SPECIFIC NOTE: IFRS-EU maintains the IAS 39 macro-hedge carve-out
(disapplying certain IAS 39 portfolio fair-value-hedge restrictions
for EU entities, primarily banks). This carve-out is NOT applicable
to trade-receivable allowances — the IFRS 9.5.5 ECL model is endorsed
in full and is identical to full IASB IFRS for trade receivables. The
carve-out is noted only for provenance; it does not affect this
treatment.
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Provision matrix (customer-adaptable DEFAULT
baseline — calibrate to entity loss history): aging-bucket loss-rate
matrix, same starting-point structure as us_gaap/ifrs_iasb T11 (0-90:
0%; 91-120: 10%; 121-150: 25%; 151-180: 50%; 181-360: 75%; 360+:
100%). This is a non-calibrated transitional placeholder; IFRS 9
requires the matrix reflect the entity's own historical loss
experience adjusted for current conditions and reasonable-and-
supportable forecasts; replace with an entity-calibrated approach;
customer-adaptable. Forward-looking adjustment — default historical
loss rates overlaid with reasonable-and-supportable factors
(required); customer-adaptable as to factors. Current-bucket (0-90) —
default 0%, flagged: the simplified approach recognizes lifetime ECL
from initial recognition, so a 0% current bucket is a customer
calibration decision, not an IFRS 9 conclusion; customer-adaptable.
applicabilityGuard: same healthcare hard-block as us_gaap/ifrs_iasb
T11 — patient service receivables are measured under IFRS 15 variable
consideration, not IFRS 9 ECL; blocked for healthcare_provider
entities; pool-level non-patient exception with specialist
attestation only.
CITATIONS: IFRS 9.5.5.15; 9.5.5.17; IFRS 7. EU endorsement:
Commission Regulation (EU) 2016/2067.
EU DIVERGENCE FROM FULL IFRS: The IAS 39 macro-hedge carve-out exists
under IFRS-EU but is not applicable to trade-receivable ECL; the ECL
spine is identical to full IASB IFRS.
## 12. ACCOUNTS PAYABLE CUTOFF AND EXPENSE RECOGNITION (SPECIFICATION)
Topic: ap_cutoff_and_expense_recognition | ifrs_eu |
extendsFrameworkDefault | displacementLineage: null
NOTE: vendor-miss detection/controller-alert is execution-layer
(roadmap RM-EXEC-3). This metadata treatment defines the cutoff
baseline.
CONFORMANT SPINE: Under the accrual basis (IAS 1.27-28, endorsed),
expenses are recognized in the period the economic benefit is
consumed (goods/services received), regardless of when the vendor
invoice is received, entered, or paid. At period end, a liability is
accrued for goods and services received but not yet invoiced or
entered (IAS 1.28; provision/liability recognition under IAS 37
where applicable). An invoice dated in one period but entered in a
subsequent period does not move the expense — the expense belongs to
the period of receipt; proper cutoff requires accruing it in the
correct period. The 2023 IAS 7/IFRS 7 supplier-finance-arrangement
amendment (endorsed) adds disclosure for reverse-factoring.
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Accrual cutoff — default at each period close,
accrue for received-but-unentered goods/services from receiving
records and known obligations (IAS 1.28); customer-adaptable.
Materiality threshold — default above an entity-defined threshold
(IAS 1.7); customer-adaptable.
EXECUTION SPEC (roadmap RM-EXEC-3): a monitoring capability should
detect invoices whose invoice date precedes their entry period,
track recurring missed vendors, and alert the controller with the
reason — human review, not auto-posting.
CITATIONS: IAS 1.7; 1.27-28; IAS 37; IAS 7/IFRS 7. EU endorsement:
Commission Regulation (EU) 2023/1803.
EU DIVERGENCE FROM FULL IFRS: None.
