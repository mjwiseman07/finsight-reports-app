## Framework: ifrs_iasb | Recommended / In-Review
OPERATING MODEL: Advisacor treatments are always recommended

## 1. PROFESSIONAL SERVICES REVENUE RECOGNITION
Topic: professional_services_revenue | ifrs_iasb |
extendsFrameworkDefault | displacementLineage: null
CONFORMANT SPINE: Revenue from professional services is recognized
under IFRS 15's five-step model in the amount the entity expects to
be entitled to (IFRS 15.2). A performance obligation is satisfied
over time if any IFRS 15.35 criterion is met: (a) customer
simultaneously receives and consumes benefits; (b) performance
creates/enhances an asset the customer controls; (c) performance
creates an asset with no alternative use AND the entity has an
enforceable right to payment for performance to date. If no
criterion is met, revenue is recognized at the point control
transfers (IFRS 15.38). Over-time progress is measured by a single
output or input method (IFRS 15.39-45). Variable consideration is
estimated (expected value or most likely amount) and constrained
where significant reversal is probable (IFRS 15.50-58). Stand-
ready/retainer arrangements that are a series of substantially-the-
same distinct services with the same progress measure are a single
performance obligation. Onerous performance obligations: IFRS
entities recognize a provision under IAS 37.66-67 (no ASC 606
parallel).
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Measure of progress — default input method
(costs/hours) where it best depicts performance; output methods
equally permitted; consistent policy disclosed (IFRS 15.123);
customer-adaptable. Variable consideration (conservative) — default
contingent/variable fees constrained to zero until the constraint
is lifted; earlier recognition routes to advised-override; customer-
adaptable. One-year expedients (IFRS 15.94 contract costs; 15.63
financing) — default applied; customer may elect not to; customer-
adaptable.
CITATIONS: IFRS 15.2; 15.9; 15.22-30; 15.35; 15.38; 15.39-45;
15.50-58; 15.63; 15.91-104; 15.123; IAS 37.66-67.
US GAAP DIVERGENCE: IFRS 15 and ASC 606 substantially converged;
onerous-contract provision (IAS 37) is the notable IFRS-only item.
## 2. INVENTORY COST-FLOW AND MEASUREMENT
Topic: smb_inventory | ifrs_iasb | extendsFrameworkDefault |
displacementLineage: null
CONFORMANT SPINE: Inventories are measured at the lower of cost and
net realisable value (IAS 2.9). Cost comprises purchase costs,
conversion costs, and other costs to bring inventory to present
location and condition (IAS 2.10-15). Permitted cost formulas are
specific identification, FIFO, or weighted-average cost only — LIFO
is PROHIBITED (IAS 2.25). The same formula applies to inventories
of similar nature and use (IAS 2.26). NRV is selling price less
costs of completion and costs to sell (IAS 2.6), assessed item-by-
item or by similar groups (IAS 2.28-29). Reversal of a prior write-
down is REQUIRED when NRV recovers, limited to the original write-
down amount (IAS 2.33) — differs from us_gaap, which prohibits
reversal.
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Cost formula — default FIFO (most widely used
under IFRS); weighted-average equally permitted; LIFO NOT available
under IFRS; customer-adaptable within the permitted set. NRV
grouping — default by item or similar-item group each reporting
date (IAS 2.29); customer-adaptable. Costing technique — default
standard or retail where it approximates actual cost (IAS 2.21-22);
customer-adaptable.
CITATIONS: IAS 2.6; 2.9-22; 2.25-29; 2.33-34; 2.36.
US GAAP DIVERGENCE: IFRS prohibits LIFO and requires NRV-write-down
reversal — two material divergences from ASC 330. Spine reflects
IFRS.
## 3. LIGHT MANUFACTURING COST / OVERHEAD ALLOCATION
Topic: light_manufacturing_cost_accounting | ifrs_iasb |
extendsFrameworkDefault | displacementLineage: null
CONFORMANT SPINE: Costs of conversion include direct labour and a
systematic allocation of fixed and variable production overheads
(IAS 2.12). Variable overhead is allocated on actual use; fixed
overhead is allocated on normal capacity — the production expected
on average over a number of periods under normal circumstances, net
of planned-maintenance capacity loss (IAS 2.13). Below normal
capacity, fixed overhead per unit is NOT increased; unabsorbed
fixed overhead is expensed in the period incurred (IAS 2.13). Above
normal capacity, fixed overhead per unit is reduced so inventory is
not measured above cost (IAS 2.13). Joint costs allocated on a
rational consistent basis; by-products typically at NRV deducted
from the main product (IAS 2.14). Service-business inventory of
unrecognized-revenue services comprises directly-engaged labour and
attributable overhead, excluding selling/admin personnel (IAS
2.19).
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Normal-capacity determination — default average
expected production over multiple periods net of planned
maintenance; entity-defined and documented; customer-adaptable.
Costing system — default actual or standard where standard
approximates actual (IAS 2.21); customer-adaptable. Joint/by-
product allocation — default relative sales value at separation;
by-products at NRV; customer-adaptable.
CITATIONS: IAS 2.12-19; 2.21.
US GAAP DIVERGENCE: Normal-capacity overhead treatment substantially
aligned with ASC 330-10-30.
## 4. DISTRIBUTOR INVENTORY AND FREIGHT
Topic: distributor_inventory_and_freight | ifrs_iasb |
extendsFrameworkDefault | displacementLineage: null
CONFORMANT SPINE: Purchased inventory cost includes purchase price,
import duties, non-recoverable taxes, transport/handling and other
directly attributable acquisition costs, less trade discounts and
rebates (IAS 2.11). Freight-in is part of cost of purchase. Selling
costs including freight-out are excluded from inventory cost (IAS
2.16) and are period costs. Inventory at lower of cost and NRV (IAS
2.9). In-transit inventory recognized per contractual transfer of
control/risks and rewards.
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Freight-in allocation — default capitalized using
a reasonable systematic method; expensing only where clearly
immaterial under a documented policy; customer-adaptable. Freight-
out — default period selling cost (IAS 2.16); customer-adaptable.
In-transit — default per shipping terms; customer-adaptable.
CITATIONS: IAS 2.10-11; 2.16; 2.9.
US GAAP DIVERGENCE: Cost-of-purchase definition substantially
aligned with ASC 330-10-30.
## 5. CONSOLIDATION AND INTERCOMPANY ELIMINATIONS
Topic: holding_company_intercompany_eliminations | ifrs_iasb |
extendsFrameworkDefault | displacementLineage: null
CONFORMANT SPINE: An investor consolidates an investee it controls.
IFRS 10 applies a SINGLE control-based model (IFRS 10.4-7) — NO
variable-interest-entity concept and no dual model as under
us_gaap. Control requires all three elements: (a) power over the
investee; (b) exposure to variable returns; (c) ability to use
power to affect those returns (IFRS 10.7). De facto control is
explicitly recognized — a large minority holder may control where
other holdings are dispersed (IFRS 10.B41-B46), so consolidation
can be required below 50% ownership. Potential voting rights
currently exercisable are considered (IFRS 10.B47-B50). Principal-
vs-agent analysis applies (IFRS 10.B58-B72). On consolidation, all
intragroup balances, transactions, and unrealised intragroup profit
in assets are eliminated in full (IFRS 10.B86(c)). NCI is presented
within equity, separate from parent equity (IFRS 10.B94).
Significant-influence interests (typically 20-50%) follow the
equity method (IAS 28) — outside this treatment.
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Control assessment — default three-element test;
de-facto-control, potential-voting-rights, principal/agent are
entity-specific judgments requiring documentation, especially for
30-50% holdings and structured entities; customer-adaptable.
Investment-entity exception (IFRS 10.27-33) — default not applied
unless the entity qualifies; customer-adaptable per business model.
Unrealised-profit elimination — default full elimination until
realised externally; customer-adaptable.
CITATIONS: IFRS 10.4-7; 10.27-33; 10.B41-B50; 10.B58-B72; 10.B86;
10.B94; IAS 28.
US GAAP DIVERGENCE: Materially different from ASC 810 — no VIE
model, recognizes de facto control (consolidation possible below
50%), one control model vs two. Spine reflects IFRS, NOT a relabel.
## 6. PAYROLL AND EMPLOYEE BENEFIT ACCRUALS
Topic: smb_payroll_and_benefits_accruals | ifrs_iasb |
extendsFrameworkDefault | displacementLineage: null
CONFORMANT SPINE: Employee benefits are accounted for under IAS 19
(share-based payment is IFRS 2, outside this treatment). Short-term
benefits (settled wholly within 12 months) are recognized as an
undiscounted liability for services rendered but unpaid, and as
expense unless capitalized into an asset (IAS 19.11). Accumulating
compensated absences are accrued as employees render service
increasing entitlement, at the additional amount expected to be
paid (IAS 19.13); non-accumulating absences when the absence occurs
(IAS 19.17). Profit-sharing and bonuses are recognized when a
present legal or constructive obligation exists and a reliable
estimate can be made; a constructive obligation arises from past
practice creating a valid employee expectation (IAS 19.19-20).
Post-employment plans are classified defined-contribution (expense
contributions as service is rendered, IAS 19.51) or defined-benefit
(projected-unit-credit; remeasurements in OCI never recycled; past
service cost expensed immediately — IAS 19.55-152). DB and other
long-term benefits are specialist/actuarial — flag, typically out
of SMB scope.
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Short-term accruals — default accrued undiscounted
for services rendered but unpaid (IAS 19.11); customer-adaptable.
Bonus/profit-share — default accrued when a legal or constructive
obligation exists and is reliably estimable (IAS 19.20); customer-
adaptable. Compensated absences — default accumulating accrued as
entitlement grows, non-accumulating when taken; customer-adaptable.
DB plans — default flagged actuarial/specialist, advised-override
where material; customer-adaptable.
CITATIONS: IAS 19.8-25; 19.11; 19.13-24; 19.51-54; 19.55-152; IFRS 2.
US GAAP DIVERGENCE: Short-term and DC accruals substantially aligned.
DB plan accounting diverges materially but DB is typically out of
SMB scope.
## 7. FIXED ASSET CATEGORIES, DEPRECIATION, AND REVALUATION
Topic: smb_fixed_asset_categories | ifrs_iasb |
extendsFrameworkDefault | displacementLineage: null
CONFORMANT SPINE: PP&E is recognized at cost when future economic
benefits are probable and cost is reliably measurable (IAS 16.7,
16.15). Initial cost includes purchase price, directly attributable
costs, and estimated dismantlement/restoration costs (IAS 16.16).
Component depreciation is MANDATORY — each significant part with a
different useful life or consumption pattern must be depreciated
separately (IAS 16.43); composite depreciation is not acceptable
where components have materially different lives. Subsequent
measurement uses, by class, the cost model (IAS 16.30) or the
revaluation model (revalued amount = fair value less subsequent
depreciation/impairment, IAS 16.31). The revaluation model is
PERMITTED under IFRS (unlike us_gaap); if elected, the entire class
must be revalued (IAS 16.36), increases to OCI as revaluation
surplus, decreases to P&L (IAS 16.39-40). Depreciable amount (cost
less residual value) is allocated over useful life; residual value
and useful life reviewed at least annually (IAS 16.50-51). Land is
not depreciated (IAS 16.58). Impairment under IAS 36: assess for
indicators each reporting date; if present, write down to
recoverable amount (higher of value-in-use and fair value less
costs to sell, IAS 36.6, 36.60). Impairment reversals are PERMITTED
up to the amount that would have been recognized absent impairment
(IAS 36.114) — unlike us_gaap, which prohibits reversal.
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Measurement model — default cost model (used by
the vast majority of IFRS entities); revaluation model available by
class if elected (IAS 16.29); customer-adaptable. Componentization
threshold — default components above an entity-defined significance
threshold depreciated separately (mandatory where significant);
customer-adaptable as to threshold, not the mandatory principle.
Useful lives/residual/method — default per documented policy;
straight-line unless another method better reflects consumption;
reviewed annually; customer-adaptable.
CITATIONS: IAS 16.7; 16.15-16; 16.29-42; 16.43-47; 16.50-62; 16.58;
IAS 36.6; 36.60; 36.114; IAS 23.
US GAAP DIVERGENCE: Three material divergences from ASC 360 — IFRS
permits revaluation (us_gaap prohibits), mandates component
depreciation (us_gaap doesn't), permits impairment reversal (us_gaap
prohibits). Spine reflects IFRS, NOT a relabel.
## 8. PREPAID / ACCRUAL CONVENTIONS
Topic: smb_prepaid_and_accrual_conventions | ifrs_iasb |
extendsFrameworkDefault | displacementLineage: null
CONFORMANT SPINE: Financial statements (except cash flow
information) are prepared on the accrual basis (IAS 1.27-28): items
are recognized when they meet Conceptual Framework recognition
criteria, not when cash moves. A prepaid expense meets the asset
definition — a right to future economic benefit (CF 4.3) —
recognized as an asset and released to expense as the benefit is
consumed. An accrued liability is recognized when a present
obligation exists for goods/services received but not yet invoiced
(CF 5.25). Expenses relating to the current period are accrued
regardless of invoice or payment date (IAS 1.28). Prepaids are
current assets if realized within the operating cycle or 12 months;
accrued liabilities current if settled within the operating cycle
or 12 months (IAS 1.54-55). The 2024 IAS 1 amendment clarifies the
right to defer settlement must have substance at the reporting date.
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Prepaid release pattern — default matched to
benefit consumption; straight-line for time-based, usage-based where
appropriate (IAS 1.28); customer-adaptable. Accrual estimation —
default from known obligations and run-rate/percentage-of-period for
unbilled items; entity policy; customer-adaptable. Materiality
threshold — default above an entity-defined threshold (IAS 1.7);
customer-adaptable.
CITATIONS: IAS 1.7; 1.27-28; 1.54-55; CF 4.3; CF 5.25-5.31; IAS
19.11.
US GAAP DIVERGENCE: Accrual and prepaid conventions substantially
aligned. (IFRS 18 replaces IAS 1 effective 2027, accrual basis
carried forward unchanged.)
## 9. DEFERRED REVENUE / CONTRACT LIABILITIES
Topic: basic_smb_deferred_revenue | ifrs_iasb |
extendsFrameworkDefault | displacementLineage: null
CONFORMANT SPINE: Under IFRS 15, the balance sheet presents a
contract liability or contract asset depending on the relationship
between performance and payment (IFRS 15.105). A contract liability
(the IFRS-15 term for deferred revenue) is the entity's obligation
to transfer goods/services for which it has received or is due
consideration (IFRS 15.106) — arising when the customer pays before
performance. A contract asset is a conditional right to
consideration, distinguished from a trade receivable, which is an
unconditional right requiring only passage of time (IFRS 15.107-
108). Within a single contract, a contract asset and contract
liability are presented net (IFRS 15.105). The contract liability is
released to revenue as the related performance obligation is
satisfied (IFRS 15.31-38). Contract assets are subject to IFRS 9 ECL
impairment (IFRS 15.107; IFRS 9.5.5.15 — see Treatment 11).
Significant financing component applies where payment is materially
in advance, with the one-year expedient (IFRS 15.60-65).
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Recognition pattern — default contract liability
released ratably for stand-ready/subscription, at point of
satisfaction for discrete; customer-adaptable. Balance-sheet
aggregation — default net per contract; granularity a judgment
(IAS 1 / IFRS 15.113); customer-adaptable. Significant financing
component — default not imputed under 12 months (15.63); assessed
above where significant; customer-adaptable.
CITATIONS: IFRS 15.105-108; 15.113; 15.116; 15.120-122; 15.60-65;
IFRS 9.5.5.15.
US GAAP DIVERGENCE: Contract-liability/contract-asset framework
converged with ASC 606. Contract-asset impairment uses IFRS 9 vs
ASC 326 — see Treatment 11.
## 10. LEASE CLASSIFICATION AND RECOGNITION (SPECIFICATION)
Topic: smb_lease_classification | ifrs_iasb |
extendsFrameworkDefault | displacementLineage: null
NOTE: SPECIFICATION of the IFRS 16 recognition model. Actual
measurement/calculation is execution-layer (roadmap RM-EXEC-1).
CONFORMANT SPINE: IFRS 16 applies a SINGLE on-balance-sheet lessee
model — NO operating-vs-finance classification for lessees (unlike
us_gaap's dual model). A lessee recognizes a right-of-use asset and
a lease liability for all leases over 12 months, subject to
exemptions (IFRS 16.22). Two optional exemptions by class/lease:
short-term leases (<=12 months, IFRS 16.5(a)) and low-value assets
(BC references ~USD 5,000 when new, IFRS 16.5(b)) — the low-value
exemption has no us_gaap equivalent. The lease liability is the
present value of unpaid lease payments at the rate implicit in the
lease, or the incremental borrowing rate if not determinable (IFRS
16.26-27). The ROU asset is the liability plus payments at/before
commencement, plus initial direct costs and restoration costs, less
incentives (IFRS 16.24). Subsequently the liability accretes
interest and reduces for payments; the ROU asset is depreciated over
the shorter of lease term and asset useful life (IFRS 16.29-33). The
income-statement effect is front-loaded (interest + depreciation)
for all lessee leases — unlike us_gaap operating leases (single
straight-line). Lessors retain finance/operating classification
(IFRS 16.61-97). Modifications (IFRS 16.44-46) and sale-and-leaseback
(IFRS 16.98-103, 2024 amendment restricting retained-ROU gains)
apply.
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Lease-term/reasonably-certain options — default
per IFRS 16.19 judgment; customer-adaptable. Short-term and low-
value exemptions — default available; customer elects by asset class
with documented rationale; customer-adaptable. Discount rate —
default implicit rate where determinable else incremental borrowing
rate (IFRS 16.26); customer-adaptable. Lease-vs-service/identified
asset — default assessed per IFRS 16.9-11; customer-adaptable.
CITATIONS: IFRS 16.5; 16.9-11; 16.19; 16.22-33; 16.44-46; 16.61-97;
16.98-103.
US GAAP DIVERGENCE: Materially different from ASC 842 — no lessee
operating/finance split (all lessee leases front-loaded), low-value
exemption us_gaap lacks. Spine reflects IFRS, NOT a relabel. Lessor
accounting remains aligned.
## 11. TRADE RECEIVABLES ALLOWANCE / EXPECTED CREDIT LOSSES (SPECIFICATION)
Topic: generic_smb_ar_allowance_ecl | ifrs_iasb |
extendsFrameworkDefault | displacementLineage: null
NOTE: SPECIFICATION for the execution-layer ECL computation engine
(roadmap RM-EXEC-2). This metadata treatment defines the model; it
performs no calculation.
CONFORMANT SPINE: Trade receivables and contract assets carry an
allowance for expected credit losses under IFRS 9. IFRS 9 uses a
forward-looking expected-credit-loss model. For trade receivables
(and contract assets) without a significant financing component, the
SIMPLIFIED APPROACH is required — lifetime ECL is recognized from
initial recognition, with no staging assessment (IFRS 9.5.5.15).
This differs from us_gaap CECL (ASC 326) in mechanics: IFRS 9's
general model is staged (12-month vs lifetime ECL based on
significant increase in credit risk), but the simplified approach
applies to trade receivables and goes straight to lifetime ECL. A
provision matrix (loss rates by aging bucket, derived from
historical loss experience and adjusted for forward-looking/
reasonable-and-supportable information) is the standard practical
method. The allowance reduces the receivable to its expected-
recoverable amount.
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Provision matrix (customer-adaptable DEFAULT
baseline — calibrate to entity loss history): aging-bucket loss-rate
matrix, same starting-point structure as us_gaap T11 (0-90: 0%;
91-120: 10%; 121-150: 25%; 151-180: 50%; 181-360: 75%; 360+: 100%).
This is a non-calibrated transitional placeholder; IFRS 9 requires
the matrix reflect the entity's own historical loss experience
adjusted for current conditions and reasonable-and-supportable
forecasts; replace with an entity-calibrated approach; customer-
adaptable. Forward-looking adjustment — default historical loss
rates overlaid with reasonable-and-supportable factors (required,
not optional); customer-adaptable as to factors not the requirement.
Current-bucket (0-90) — default 0%, flagged: the simplified approach
recognizes lifetime ECL from initial recognition, so a 0% current
bucket is a customer calibration decision, not an IFRS 9 conclusion;
customer-adaptable.
applicabilityGuard: same healthcare hard-block as us_gaap T11 —
patient service receivables are measured under IFRS 15 variable
consideration, not IFRS 9 ECL; blocked for healthcare_provider
entities; pool-level non-patient exception with specialist
attestation only.
CITATIONS: IFRS 9.5.5.15; 9.5.5.17; IFRS 7.
US GAAP DIVERGENCE: IFRS 9 ECL differs from ASC 326 CECL in
mechanics — IFRS 9 has a staged general model but a mandatory
simplified (lifetime-ECL-from-inception) approach for trade
receivables; us_gaap CECL applies lifetime expected loss without
staging. Provision-matrix method and forward-looking requirement are
common ground; conceptual basis differs; spine cites IFRS 9.
## 12. ACCOUNTS PAYABLE CUTOFF AND EXPENSE RECOGNITION (SPECIFICATION)
Topic: ap_cutoff_and_expense_recognition | ifrs_iasb |
extendsFrameworkDefault | displacementLineage: null
NOTE: vendor-miss detection/controller-alert is execution-layer
(roadmap RM-EXEC-3). This metadata treatment defines the cutoff
baseline.
CONFORMANT SPINE: Under the accrual basis (IAS 1.27-28), expenses
are recognized in the period the economic benefit is consumed
(goods/services received), regardless of when the vendor invoice is
received, entered, or paid. At period end, a liability is accrued
for goods and services received but not yet invoiced or entered (IAS
1.28; provision/liability recognition under IAS 37 / the Conceptual
Framework where applicable). An invoice dated in one period but
entered in a subsequent period does not move the expense — the
expense belongs to the period of receipt; proper cutoff requires
accruing it in the correct period. The 2023 IAS 7/IFRS 7 supplier-
finance-arrangement amendment adds disclosure for reverse-factoring.
ADAPTABLE JOINTS: Accrual cutoff — default at each period close,
accrue for received-but-unentered goods/services from receiving
records and known obligations (IAS 1.28); customer-adaptable.
Materiality threshold — default above an entity-defined threshold
(IAS 1.7); customer-adaptable.
EXECUTION SPEC (roadmap RM-EXEC-3): a monitoring capability should
detect invoices whose invoice date precedes their entry period,
track recurring missed vendors, and alert the controller with the
reason — human review, not auto-posting.
CITATIONS: IAS 1.7; 1.27-28; IAS 37; IAS 7/IFRS 7.
US GAAP DIVERGENCE: Accrual and cutoff principles substantially
aligned.
